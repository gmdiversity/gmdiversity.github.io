Marketing for Millennials

As millennials become an increasing target consumer for automakers around the country, General Motors hones in on the little details to make its products more appealing for the demographic

by Benjamin Din, Gabrielle Settles and Wyatt Salsbury

At the 2016 North American International Auto Show, automakers presented their newest and greatest designs. From the Buick Avista to the Chevrolet Bolt EV, General Motors demonstrated an increased desire to make itself more marketable to millennials.

millennials: Data released in June 2015 indicated there were 83.1 million millennials in America, defined as youth born between 1982 and 2000.

More than a quarter of American youth are millennials, according to U.S. Census Bureau data. It comes as no surprise that companies such as General Motors are increasingly expanding their marketing strategies to focus on millennials.

At the 2016 North American International Auto Show in Detroit, GM unveiled several new car models, including its Buick Avista concept car and the production model of the Chevrolet Bolt EV.

Steve Majoros: Marketing director for Chevrolet Cars & Crossovers, since March 2014. Majoros began working at GM in April 2013 in the same position at Cadillac.

The newer car models are all attempts to attract a younger generation through a variety of methods, including sleek designs and brand repositioning. The key to marketing GM cars is making sure millennials are aware and have a positive inclination toward GM brands, said Steve Majoros, marketing director at Chevrolet.

"We have to put ourselves in a position where they feel good about the Chevrolet brand," Majoros said. "We just don't have the brand awareness (in some areas), so it's about who we are and creating a brand identity for ourselves."

Millennials – who are expected to have an annual spending power of $200 billion by 2017 – are a growing market, and April 2015 data from J.D. Power & Associates showed they represented 27 percent of new cars sales.

For many, this comes as a shock. A common misperception is that young people are not interested in buying new cars; however, data shows that millennials have outpaced Generation X and is only second to Boomers in new car sales.

From design to engineering to marketing, GM has realized the necessity of reaching out to the growing market of millennials.
